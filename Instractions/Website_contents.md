---

### **What is the lowest I can spend on Google Ads?**

Google Ads has no fixed minimum spend; the platform lets you set any daily budget you choose. However, a practical starting floor for most businesses is $10 to $20 per day. If your budget is genuinely tight, the most effective approach is to focus on one or two highly specific, exact-match keywords in a small geographic area rather than spreading a thin budget across many keywords. Concentration beats distribution when the budget is limited; every dollar needs to go toward the highest-intent searches most likely to convert.

---

### **Is $100 enough for Google Ads?**

$100 can get you started, but how far it goes depends entirely on your industry and keyword CPCs. A useful budgeting approach is to estimate your average cost per conversion and multiply it by ten, which gives you a reasonable starting monthly budget. So if a single conversion costs you around $10 on average, $100 per month is a workable proof-of-concept test. However, in most competitive markets, $100 translates to just three to five clicks per month, nowhere near enough to identify meaningful patterns or generate consistent leads. For small businesses aiming for real results, a starting budget of around $20 per day, approximately $600 per month, is a more realistic baseline. Think of $100 as an experiment, not a lead generation strategy.
